 📌 Project Overview
An interactive **HR Summary Dashboard** built in Tableau to help HR managers 
get a high-level view of workforce data across hiring, attrition, demographics, 
and salary — all in one place.

Built end-to-end following a real-world project workflow:
**Business Requirements → Mockups → Data Preparation → Dashboard Delivery**


 🎯 Business Problem
HR managers lacked a centralized view of workforce health.
This dashboard consolidates key HR metrics — headcount, attrition, 
demographics, and compensation — to support faster, data-driven 
decisions around hiring and retention strategy.


 ✅ Dashboard Features

### 👥 Overview Section
- Total hired **(8,950)**, active **(7,984)** & terminated **(966)** employees
- Hiring and termination trends visualized over the years
- Employee breakdown across **7 departments**
- HQ vs Branch employee headcount comparison
- Geographic distribution across US cities and states

### 🧑‍🤝‍🧑 Demographics Section
- Gender ratio — **54% Male, 46% Female**
- Age group distribution across education levels
- Largest segment — **Bachelor's degree holders aged 35–44 (18%)**
- Education vs Performance rating heatmap

### 💰 Income Analysis Section
- Salary progression by education:
  **H-Sch: ~63K → Bachelor: ~66-74K → Master: ~80-86K → PhD: ~80-93K**
- Gender-based salary comparison across education levels
- Age vs Salary scatter plot by job role


## 💡 Key Insights
- 🏢 Out of 8,950 total employees, 7,984 are active and 966 terminated
  — reflecting a **~10.8% attrition rate**
- 🏭 **Operations** is the largest department, followed by Sales and 
  Customer Service — HR is the smallest
- 📍 Majority of employees are in **HQ**, with **New York** as the 
  primary state
- 👥 Workforce is nearly gender-balanced — **54% Male, 46% Female**
- 🎓 Bachelor's degree holders aged **35–44** form the largest 
  employee segment at 18%
- 💰 Salary scales significantly with education — PhD holders 
  earn up to **93K** vs 63K for High School graduates
- ⚧️ A **gender pay gap** exists at Bachelor level — males earn 
  ~74K vs females ~66K on average
- 📈 **Finance Manager** is the highest paid role, followed by 
  IT Manager and HR Manager
- 🎯 Master's and PhD holders show the highest proportion of 
  **"Excellent"** performance ratings
